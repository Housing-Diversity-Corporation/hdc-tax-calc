# HDC Platform Strategy Summary

## HDC Integrated Platform Model

### Overview
HDC operates as both developer and capital provider, capturing value at every level without equity risk.

### Two Business Lines:

#### 1. HDC Debt Fund (HDC DF)
- **Purpose**: Gap financing to reach 95% LTV/LTC
- **Position**: 20-35% of capital stack (only what senior lenders won't provide)
- **Rate**: 11-12% interest
- **Philosophy**: Never displaces cheaper capital - aligned with investors

#### 2. HDC Development Platform (Zero Equity)
Fee-based income without equity investment:
- Development fees: 3-5% upfront
- AUM fees: 1-2% annually (Years 2-10)
- Tax benefit fees: 10% of depreciation benefits
- Promote: 65% of profits at exit

### Combined Platform Economics

**Example $100M Project:**
- HDC Investment: $30M (debt only)
- HDC Returns: ~$90M over 10 years
- Platform IRR: ~25%
- Multiple: 3.0x

### Key Strategic Advantages:

1. **No Equity Risk**: HDC has zero equity exposure
2. **Infinite Leverage**: Unlimited upside on $0 equity investment
3. **Self-Funding**: Debt income covers operating shortfalls
4. **Scalable**: Limited only by debt capital, not equity
5. **Aligned Interests**: Lower blended debt costs benefit all parties

### Integration with RetireBetterOZ

OZ investors provide the 5% equity slice through RetireBetterOZ platform:
- Investors get tax benefits + returns
- HDC gets promote without investing
- Creates pipeline of qualified OZ projects

### HDC DF Intelligent Positioning

Dynamic gap filling based on available capital:
```
If senior lender provides 75% → HDC DF only needs 10%
If senior lender provides 60% → HDC DF provides 35%
If land acquisition → HDC DF can provide up to 80%
```

### Bottom Line
HDC achieves private equity returns (25% IRR) with lending risk profile, creating a sustainable affordable housing development machine.
